Overview

The Milky Way is the barred spiral galaxy containing our Solar System. It is approximately 100,000 light-years in diameter and contains an estimated 100-400 billion stars. The Sun orbits the galactic center at roughly 26,000 light-years out, between the Sagittarius and Perseus spiral arms in a region called the Orion Spur.

Active missions

Gaia (ESA)
Launched 2013, operations 2014-2025
Precision astrometry of ~1 billion stars. Revolutionized our 3D map of the Milky Way. DR3 data released 2022.
Hipparcos (ESA)
1989-1993
Predecessor to Gaia. Catalogued 118,000 stars to milliarcsecond precision.
Event Horizon Telescope
First Sgr A* image 2022
Global VLBI array. Captured the first image of Sagittarius A* at the galactic center.

Notable features

Spiral arms
Four major arms: Scutum-Centaurus, Perseus, Sagittarius, and Norma. Plus the Orion Spur where Sol resides.
Galactic bar
Central bar structure ~27,000 light-years long, made of older red stars.
Sagittarius A*
Supermassive black hole at the galactic center. Imaged by the Event Horizon Telescope in 2022.
Galactic halo
Diffuse sphere of old stars, globular clusters, and dark matter extending far beyond the disk.
Satellite galaxies
Large and Small Magellanic Clouds are the most prominent satellite galaxies. Being actively cannibalized.
Andromeda collision
Expected collision/merger with M31 (Andromeda Galaxy) in ~4.5 billion years.
